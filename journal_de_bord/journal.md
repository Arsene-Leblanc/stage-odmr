\### 2026/06/17

First attemps with a new sample preparation method. The method consists in melting PTP directly, adding 0.1% (solid) pentacene. Temperature can then be raised above the pentacene metling point too, or it is also possible to try to dissolve the pentacene in the PTP melt(by stirring) without melting it itself. We then try to collect the resulting melt between 2 lamelles by capillarity. We did see some of the pentacene dissolve into some pockets of PTP, but one problem of the method is that there is a strong tendency for PTP to crystalize, especially on the corners of the beaker. Here it doesn't help that the bottom of the beaker is in oval shape, with gravity tending to bring any melt near the corners. Another problem is that some PTP is also turning into vapour, which then condensates e.g. near colder surfaces and either crystalize on the surfaces or rains down in crystal form(the grains acting as potential seeds).
